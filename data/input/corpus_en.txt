Distributed systems are designed to process tasks across multiple machines. 
Parallel workers can improve throughput, but they also introduce challenges such as synchronization and consistency.

A translation pipeline must preserve meaning across sections. 
When multiple workers translate different chunks independently, technical terms may become inconsistent.

To solve this, a shared glossary can be used. 
Each worker consults the glossary before translating and may suggest new terms to be added.

Retries and backoff are important when external services fail. 
Message queues help decouple the orchestrator from the workers.

Structured logs make it possible to reconstruct the execution flow. 
Metrics such as latency, throughput, token usage, and error rate are essential for evaluation.
